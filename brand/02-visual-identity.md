# Grupo Aia — Visual Identity System

---

## 1. The Logo — Analysis & System

### The Existing Mark

The Aia logo is a **handwritten signature script** in warm gold on a rich black ground, with the three pillar words — ALMA INTEGRAÇÃO ACOLHIMENTO — set below in a clean, tracked, light-weight sans-serif in white.

### Logo Anatomy

**The Signature Script — "Aia"**
- A flowing, calligraphic handwritten form with a sweeping horizontal stroke extending from the capital A
- The A's descending stroke connects fluidly into the lowercase "ia," creating a single continuous gesture — the pen never lifts
- The extended horizontal sweep below the text acts as a grounding line, a signature flourish, and a visual foundation for the pillar words above
- The script has a textured, matte gold foil quality — warm, not metallic-bright

**The Pillar Words — "ALMA INTEGRAÇÃO ACOLHIMENTO"**
- Set in all caps, light-weight sans-serif (appears to be a geometric or humanist sans)
- Generous letter-spacing (+0.15em–0.20em), creating breathing room and elegance
- White on dark ground — clean, modern contrast to the organic script above
- Arranged in two lines: "ALMA INTEGRAÇÃO" / "ACOLHIMENTO" — centered beneath the script

### Strategic Analysis of the Logo

**What it communicates:**
- **The signature** = personal, intimate, human. This is not an institution — it's a relationship. Signatures carry trust, identity, and the physical gesture of a hand
- **The continuous stroke** = integration (Integração). Nothing is separate — the letters flow as one gesture, just as the brand promises to treat the whole person
- **The gold** = soul (Alma), inner light, wisdom, warmth. Matte gold, not shiny — genuine warmth, not performance
- **The rich black** = depth, gravitas, the container for the soul's light. Not clinical white, not corporate blue — the darkness that makes the gold visible
- **The sweep/flourish** = Acolhimento — the welcoming gesture, the open arm, the line that extends beyond the word itself to create space for others

**What it avoids:**
- No clinical or healthcare visual codes (crosses, hearts, leaves)
- No corporate rigidity (perfect geometry, sharp edges)
- No youthful energy (bright colors, sans-serif logos)
- The result is a brand that reads as luxury-warm, not medical — closer to a fine hotel or private practice than a health system

### Logo Element: The Signature Sweep

The extended horizontal stroke from the capital A becomes the brand's primary graphic element (replacing the previously proposed arc). This sweep is the most distinctive and ownable element of the logo — it can be extracted and used as:
- A section divider (single horizontal sweep)
- A framing element (anchored to corners of cards, images, containers)
- A transition element in presentations and video
- A background texture (repeated at low opacity)
- A signature mark for social media avatars and favicons (the initial sweep of the A)

## 2. Logo Variations

### Primary Logo — Gold on Black (Default)
- Full logo: Script "Aia" in Signature Gold (#B8964B) + pillar words in White (#FFFFFF) on Rich Black (#1A1A1A)
- Usage: Default for all applications where dark backgrounds are available. This is the logo's natural habitat — gold light emerging from darkness.

### Primary Logo — Gold on Dark Surfaces
- Script "Aia" in Signature Gold + pillar words in White/Cream
- Background: Any sufficiently dark surface (Rich Black, Charcoal, dark photography)
- Usage: When exact brand black isn't possible but dark ground is available

### Secondary Logo — Dark on Light
- Script "Aia" in Rich Black (#1A1A1A) + pillar words in Charcoal (#2D2D2D) on white or Cream (#FAF3EC) backgrounds
- Usage: Light-background applications — letterhead, documents, light-mode digital interfaces

### Secondary Logo — Gold Monochrome
- Full logo in Signature Gold (#B8964B) only (script + pillar words)
- Usage: On dark photography, video overlays, environmental signage on dark materials

### Icon Only — The Sweep
- The initial stroke of the capital A extracted as standalone mark
- In Signature Gold on dark backgrounds, or Rich Black on light backgrounds
- Usage: Favicons, app icons, social media avatars, watermarks, brand patterns

### Monochrome — Black
- Full logo in 100% black (#000000)
- Usage: Single-color printing, fax, stamps, legal documents

### Monochrome — White (Reversed)
- Full logo in 100% white (#FFFFFF)
- Usage: On photography, video, or colored backgrounds where brand colors aren't available

### Minimum Size Specifications
- Full logo (script + pillar words): minimum width 35mm (print) / 140px (digital)
- Script only ("Aia"): minimum width 20mm (print) / 80px (digital)
- Icon only (sweep): minimum width 8mm (print) / 32px (digital)
- Below these sizes, the script's fine strokes lose legibility

### Clear Space Requirements
- Minimum clear space = height of the lowercase "i" in the script, applied on all four sides
- Measured from the outermost edge of the script flourish (top/left) and baseline of pillar words (bottom)
- No other text, graphic element, or edge of material may enter this zone
- For the icon alone, clear space = 50% of the icon's width on all sides

## 3. Logo Usage Rules

### Correct Applications

1. **Gold on Rich Black** — Primary version, the logo's signature presentation
2. **Dark on light backgrounds** — Secondary version (Rich Black script on Cream/white) for stationery, documents, and light-mode interfaces
3. **On dark photography** — Gold monochrome version on areas of low visual complexity; ensure gold maintains contrast
4. **At small sizes** — Switch to the Sweep icon below minimum full-logo size
5. **In co-branding** — Use script-only "Aia" version (no pillar words), separated from partner logo by a vertical rule with clear space respected on both sides

### Incorrect Applications — DO NOT:

1. **DO NOT rotate or skew** the logo. The script must always appear at its natural angle — it has its own built-in dynamism.
2. **DO NOT change the colors** to unapproved combinations. The gold is specific (#B8964B) — do not substitute with yellow, orange, or metallic gradients.
3. **DO NOT add effects** — no drop shadows, outer glows, bevels, or emboss effects. The textured gold quality exists only in the original artwork, not as an applied filter.
4. **DO NOT rearrange elements** — the spatial relationship between the script and the pillar words is fixed. Do not stack them differently or change alignment.
5. **DO NOT place on mid-tone backgrounds** — the logo requires either a dark ground (for gold version) or a light ground (for dark version). Gray or medium-value backgrounds kill the contrast that gives the mark its power.

## 4. Color Palette

*Derived directly from the existing logo — every color in this system traces back to the gold-on-black signature.*

### Primary Colors

#### Rich Black — "Profundidade"
- **Hex:** #1A1A1A
- **RGB:** 26, 26, 26
- **CMYK:** 0, 0, 0, 95 (use Rich Black build for offset: 40C, 30M, 30Y, 100K)
- **Pantone:** Black 6 C
- **Usage:** Primary brand color. Logo backgrounds, headers, hero sections, presentation dividers, premium printed materials.
- **Psychology:** Depth, gravitas, the unknown, containment. Rich Black is the night sky, the closed eye during meditation, the silence before a conversation begins. For Aia, it represents Profundidade — the depth from which understanding emerges. It communicates premium care and quiet authority. It is the darkness that makes the gold visible — without it, the soul's light has no contrast.

#### Signature Gold — "Alma"
- **Hex:** #B8964B
- **RGB:** 184, 150, 75
- **CMYK:** 20, 30, 75, 5
- **Pantone:** 7555 C
- **Usage:** Logo script, accent elements, highlights, decorative elements, premium CTAs.
- **Psychology:** Inner light, wisdom, warmth, value, the soul made visible. This is not bright metallic gold — it is muted, warm, earthy. The color of late afternoon sunlight on old stone, of candlelight, of something precious that has been carried a long time. It communicates the Alma — the soul — and the warmth of genuine care. Matte, not shiny. Earned, not flashy.

#### Warm White — "Clareza"
- **Hex:** #F5F0E8
- **RGB:** 245, 240, 232
- **CMYK:** 3, 3, 7, 0
- **Pantone:** 7527 C
- **Usage:** Primary light background. Print surfaces, digital light-mode backgrounds, body text on dark grounds. Replaces pure white throughout the brand.
- **Psychology:** Openness, clarity, calm. Not clinical white (too cold) or stark white (too harsh for aging eyes). This warm white carries the same golden undertone as the brand's gold — even the lightest surfaces feel connected to the soul of the brand. It is the color of quality paper, of morning light through linen curtains.

### Secondary Colors

#### Champagne — "Acolhimento"
- **Hex:** #D4C5A9
- **RGB:** 212, 197, 169
- **CMYK:** 13, 15, 30, 2
- **Pantone:** 468 C
- **Usage:** Card backgrounds, soft containers, secondary backgrounds, printed materials, environmental surfaces.
- **Psychology:** Warmth, embrace, comfort. A light gold-beige that bridges the Signature Gold and Warm White — it is the color of Acolhimento, of being received into a warm space. Not pink (too feminine-coded), not gray (too cold). This is the color of aged parchment, of sandstone, of a cashmere scarf.

#### Espresso — "Raíz"
- **Hex:** #3D2E1F
- **RGB:** 61, 46, 31
- **CMYK:** 45, 55, 65, 60
- **Pantone:** 4625 C
- **Usage:** Alternative dark ground, text on light backgrounds (warmer than pure black), illustration accents, deep environmental surfaces.
- **Psychology:** Earth, roots, heritage, depth. A warm near-black with brown undertones — the color of dark wood, espresso, rich soil. Connects to Portuguese/Brazilian material culture. Provides warmth in dark applications where Rich Black feels too severe.

#### Antique Bronze — "Sabedoria"
- **Hex:** #8B7340
- **RGB:** 139, 115, 64
- **CMYK:** 30, 35, 75, 20
- **Pantone:** 4495 C
- **Usage:** Secondary accent, chart/data visualization, icon fills, tertiary UI elements.
- **Psychology:** Wisdom, patina, time-earned value. A deeper, more subdued version of the Signature Gold — like gold that has aged gracefully. It represents Sabedoria (wisdom) and the value of experience. Useful when Signature Gold is too bright or decorative for functional contexts.

#### Blush Clay — "Presença"
- **Hex:** #C9A88C
- **RGB:** 201, 168, 140
- **CMYK:** 15, 28, 38, 4
- **Pantone:** 4665 C
- **Usage:** Warm accents, illustration fills, photography color grading anchor, seasonal campaigns.
- **Psychology:** Skin, warmth, human presence. A warm pink-terracotta that grounds the palette in the body. The gold speaks to the soul; this color speaks to the human being who carries it. Connects to Mediterranean/Portuguese ceramic and earth tones.

### Neutral Colors (UI System)

| Name | Hex | Usage |
|-|-|-|
| Deep Charcoal | #2D2D2D | Body text on light backgrounds |
| Warm Gray | #5C5549 | Secondary text, captions (warm-shifted, not blue-gray) |

**Note on neutrals:** All neutrals in this system carry a warm undertone — they are shifted toward brown/gold rather than blue/cool. This ensures that even functional UI elements feel connected to the brand's warm, earthy palette. No cool grays are used anywhere.

### Accent Colors (CTAs & Alerts)

| Name | Hex | Usage |
|-|-|-|
| Bright Gold | #D4A543 | Primary CTA buttons, active links, focus states |
| Affirm Green | #6B8F5E | Success states, positive feedback, confirmation |
| Gentle Alert | #C47A4A | Warning states, important notices (warm amber, not aggressive red) |

**Note on accents:** The primary CTA is Bright Gold — a slightly more saturated and luminous version of Signature Gold, designed for interactive elements that need to attract attention. It maintains the gold family while providing the extra contrast needed for clickable elements.

### Color Proportion Guide

- **40% Rich Black** — the dominant tone, providing depth and luxury
- **30% Warm White / Champagne** — breathing space, readability
- **20% Signature Gold** — accents, highlights, the soul's presence
- **10% Secondary colors** — warmth, variety, human dimension

### Color Accessibility Notes

- All text-on-background combinations must meet WCAG 2.1 AA standards (4.5:1 for body text, 3:1 for large text)
- Primary audience is 40–90: consider age-related vision changes. Higher contrast ratios are preferred. Target AAA (7:1) where possible
- Signature Gold (#B8964B) on Rich Black (#1A1A1A) achieves approximately 5.8:1 contrast — AA compliant for all text sizes
- Deep Charcoal (#2D2D2D) on Warm White (#F5F0E8) achieves approximately 11.2:1 — AAA compliant
- Never use color alone to convey meaning — always pair with text labels or icons
- Avoid red for error states — use Gentle Alert (#C47A4A) which is warm rather than alarming, more appropriate for a care-oriented brand
- For body text on dark backgrounds, always use Warm White (#F5F0E8), never Signature Gold (insufficient contrast at small sizes)

## 5. Typography

### Primary Typeface: Cormorant Garamond

**Classification:** Elegant serif, display-optimized
**Designer:** Christian Thalmann
**License:** Open source (Google Fonts / SIL Open Font License)

**Why Cormorant Garamond:**
- **Elegant and modern** while rooted in classical proportion — exactly the brief's request
- High contrast between thick and thin strokes creates visual sophistication
- The tall x-height and open counters ensure readability even at smaller sizes — critical for the 40–90 audience
- The italic is genuinely beautiful — calligraphic without being decorative — useful for pull quotes and emotional messaging
- It carries literary, intellectual energy (Sage archetype) without feeling academic or distant
- Portuguese/Latin typographic heritage — feels culturally aligned
- Available in weights from Light to Bold, providing full hierarchy flexibility

**Weights used:**
- **Cormorant Garamond Light** — Taglines, large display text (48pt+)
- **Cormorant Garamond Regular** — Headlines, subheadings (24–48pt)
- **Cormorant Garamond Medium** — Logo wordmark, emphasized text
- **Cormorant Garamond SemiBold** — Section titles, navigation
- **Cormorant Garamond Bold** — Sparingly, for critical emphasis only

### Secondary Typeface: Inter

**Classification:** Humanist sans-serif, optimized for screens
**Designer:** Rasmus Andersson
**License:** Open source (SIL Open Font License)

**Why Inter:**
- Designed specifically for computer screens — excellent rendering at all sizes
- Tall x-height and open apertures maximize legibility for aging eyes
- Tabular and oldstyle figure options for data-heavy contexts
- 9 weights provide granular control over hierarchy
- Neutral enough to let Cormorant Garamond be the star, but warm enough to not feel generic
- Extensive language support including Portuguese diacritics

**Weights and usage:**

| Level | Typeface | Weight | Size (Digital) | Size (Print) |
|-|-|-|-|-|
| Display | Cormorant Garamond | Light | 48–72px | 36–60pt |
| H1 | Cormorant Garamond | Regular | 36–48px | 28–36pt |
| H2 | Cormorant Garamond | Medium | 28–36px | 22–28pt |
| H3 | Cormorant Garamond | SemiBold | 22–28px | 18–22pt |
| H4 | Inter | SemiBold | 18–22px | 14–18pt |
| Body | Inter | Regular | 16–18px | 11–12pt |
| Body emphasis | Inter | Medium | 16–18px | 11–12pt |
| Caption | Inter | Regular | 13–14px | 9–10pt |
| Overline | Inter | SemiBold, uppercase, tracked | 11–12px | 8–9pt |
| Button | Inter | Medium | 14–16px | 10–11pt |

### Typography Rules

1. **Minimum body text size: 16px digital, 11pt print.** Our audience ranges to 90 years old. Readability is non-negotiable.
2. **Line height: 1.5–1.7 for body text.** Generous leading aids readability for all ages.
3. **Line length: 60–75 characters maximum.** Prevents eye fatigue.
4. **Letter-spacing for uppercase: +0.05em minimum.** All-caps text must be tracked for legibility.
5. **Never use Cormorant below 18px/12pt.** Its high-contrast strokes break down at small sizes. Switch to Inter.
6. **Paragraph spacing: 1em.** Clear visual separation between blocks.
7. **Avoid justified text.** Ragged right is more readable, especially for older readers.

## 6. Imagery Style

### Photography Guidelines

**Mood:** Contemplative, warm, dignified. Every image should feel like a moment of quiet presence — not posed happiness, not clinical observation.

**Lighting:**
- Predominantly natural light — warm, golden-hour quality
- Soft shadows, never harsh or dramatic
- Avoid overhead fluorescent lighting (reads as clinical)
- Indoor scenes should feel sun-lit through windows, not studio-lit

**Subjects:**
- Real people aged 40–90, diverse in ethnicity, body type, and ability
- Individuals in moments of reflection, connection, or gentle activity
- Hands featured prominently — holding, touching, gesturing (hands tell the story of a life lived)
- Couples and groups in authentic interaction, not staged groupings
- Environments: gardens, well-lit interiors, natural settings, reading nooks, kitchens — spaces of daily life, not clinical spaces

**Composition:**
- Rule of thirds with subject often positioned at the edges, looking inward — draws the viewer into the space
- Negative space is welcome — it mirrors the breathing room the brand promises
- Shallow depth of field to create intimacy
- Avoid centered, symmetrical compositions (too formal/corporate)
- Crop generously — partial views of faces, hands, and spaces create intimacy and curiosity

**Color treatment:**
- Warm color grading aligned with brand palette
- Slightly desaturated — never over-processed or HDR
- Shadows lifted gently (no true blacks in lifestyle photography)
- Consistent across all applications — create a Lightroom/Capture One preset

**DO NOT use:**
- Stock photography cliches (clasped hands on desk, stethoscopes, fake smiles)
- Images that infantilize older adults (being helped when not needed, looking frail)
- Clinical or hospital settings
- People looking directly at camera with corporate smiles
- Overly filtered or Instagram-aesthetic treatment

### Illustration Style

**When to use:** For concepts that photography cannot capture — internal states, metaphors, abstract processes.

**Style:** Organic line illustration with watercolor-wash fills.
- Single continuous line where possible (integration metaphor)
- Line weight: 1.5–2pt, slightly irregular (human, not mechanical)
- Color fills from the gold/warm palette (Signature Gold, Champagne, Blush Clay), applied loosely — not contained by the line
- Botanical and natural motifs preferred (leaves, water, light, roots, birds)
- Abstract representations of human forms — suggested, not detailed

### Iconography Style

**Line weight:** 1.5px consistent stroke
**Corner radius:** 2px (slightly rounded, never sharp, never fully circular)
**Grid:** 24x24px base grid with 2px padding
**Fill rules:** Outline only for navigation/UI icons. Filled for status/action icons.
**Style:** Rounded joins, rounded caps. Organic but precise.
**Palette:** Single color from neutrals (Deep Charcoal or Warm Gray) in standard state. Signature Gold or Bright Gold for active/selected states.

### Graphic Element: The Signature Sweep

The extending horizontal stroke from the logo's capital A becomes the brand's primary graphic element:
- Used as section dividers (single horizontal sweep in Signature Gold, or Champagne at low opacity)
- As background patterns (repeated sweeps at 3–5% opacity on dark or light grounds)
- As frame elements (anchored to corners of cards, images, containers — the sweep entering from the left edge)
- As transition elements in presentations and video (gold sweep wiping across)
- As underline accents beneath headlines (a subtle nod to the logo's signature quality)

The sweep is always drawn from the gold family (Signature Gold, Antique Bronze, or Bright Gold depending on context) and never competes with content. It is felt, not seen — the gesture of a hand that has been here before you.
